SOVIET CULTURAL GATEKEEPING ANALYSIS

Bayesian ML for Cold War Literary Translation

PROBLEM_STATEMENT.txt
  • HISTORICAL_QUESTION: How did Soviet journals select American literature for translation during Cold War (1950-1991)?
  • TWO_JOURNALS: Inostrannaia Literatura (IL) vs. Vsesvit
  • DATASET: 467 records | 183 authors | 5 decades | 5 gen_secretaries
  • RESEARCH_OBJECTIVES:
    • Stage_1: Predict publication probability p(Published | Author, Decade, Regime)
    • Stage_2: Predict journal selection p(Journal | Published, Context)
    • Quantify ideology vs. literary_merit in editorial decisions

BAYESIAN NETWORK ARCHITECTURE

NETWORK_TOPOLOGY.dag
STAGE_1: PUBLICATION_DECISION
ROOT NODES:
Decade (D)
Author (A)
D --> GS     D --> P     A --> P
INTERMEDIATE:
General Secretary (GS)
GS --> P
TARGET:
Published (P) [Yes/No]
P depends on: D (direct) + GS + A
Dependencies: D --> GS,   GS --> P,   D --> P,   A --> P
P(D, GS, A, P) = p(D) * p(GS|D) * p(A) * p(P|D,GS,A)
IF Published (P = 1) THEN:
V   V   V
STAGE_2: JOURNAL_SELECTION
PARENT NODES:
Gen. Secretary (GS)
Decade (D)
Author (A)
GS --> J     D --> J     A --> J
TARGET:
Journal (J) [IL / Vsesvit]
J depends on: GS + D + A
Dependencies: GS --> J,   D --> J,   A --> J
P(J | D, GS, A, P=1)
INSIGHT: Two-stage model separates REGIME_CENSORSHIP (what gets published) from EDITORIAL_TASTE (where it appears). Enables causal reasoning about Soviet cultural policy.
